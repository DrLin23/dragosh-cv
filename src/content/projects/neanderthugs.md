Built with Andres Lot and Marcel Niedzielski for Mini Jam 207 under the "Primal"
theme (with the added constraint "Become the villain"). We placed #27 overall
and #18 for presentation out of the submissions.

## What the game is

A top-down prehistoric brawler where a revenge storyline pushes the player past
the line the rest of the village wouldn't cross. The loop runs across a handful
of levels and closes on a proper end state rather than trailing off, which is
the unusual thing for a 72-hour jam.

## What was in scope

Within the three days we got dialogue, pathfinding, pickup items, multiple
levels, a menu system, and an end state all landing together. The focus of my
contribution was on the systems side — state handling, level flow, and the bits
that keep the game feeling like a whole thing rather than a demo reel.

## What I'd do differently

Scoping would still be the hardest problem. Most features that survived the jam
were the ones that were cut down early and then iterated on, and the ones that
nearly broke us were the ones we defended for too long. Next jam I'd rather
ship an ugly whole than a polished half.
